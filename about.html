About The Project

Discover the research and technology behind XrayDx.

01

The Challenge

Bone fracture diagnosis is often time-sensitive and highly dependent on expert interpretation. Subtle fracture patterns can be easily overlooked, especially in high-volume clinical settings. XrayDx explores how AI can assist healthcare professionals by providing rapid and reliable fracture localization from X-ray images.

02

Understanding Existing Approaches

We reviewed leading fracture detection architectures including YOLOv8-AM, WCAY, YOLOv11-GAM, and FracFusionNet. Studying these systems helped us understand modern attention mechanisms, feature extraction strategies, and the current limitations of automated fracture detection.

03

Building the Foundation

To train and evaluate our models, we explored several publicly available medical imaging datasets including GRAZPEDWRI-DX, FracAtlas, BFMRX, and other fracture-focused radiographic datasets. After evaluating annotation quality, dataset diversity, and suitability for object detection, we selected high-quality fracture datasets containing expert-labeled X-ray images.

04

From Baselines to Innovation

Our research began with reproducing and analyzing baseline YOLO-based fracture detection models. These experiments provided valuable insights into small-object detection, multi-scale feature learning, and the unique challenges presented by pediatric wrist radiographs.

05

YOLOv11 + Dynamic Snake Convolution + ResCBAM

Our proposed architecture combines YOLOv11 with Dynamic Snake Convolution and ResCBAM attention modules. This design enhances feature extraction, improves focus on fracture-relevant regions, and enables more adaptive learning of subtle bone fracture patterns.

06

Building XrayDx

To make our research accessible, we developed XrayDx as a modern web platform for AI-assisted fracture detection. The system combines advanced computer vision models with an intuitive interface that allows users to upload and analyze X-ray images effortlessly.

07

Future Scope & Research Impact

Developed during a Summer Research Internship at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, this project investigates a novel fracture detection pipeline built on YOLOv11, DSC, and ResCBAM. The architecture presents promising opportunities for future research in medical imaging and AI-assisted diagnosis.
